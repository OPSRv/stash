01 · Philosophy

A deliberate «Frankenstein»
beats a zoo of dock apps.

A real day isn't one full-screen app. It's a dozen tiny actions a minute: translate, copy, grab a video, start a timer. Stash gives them a single address.

I.

UI / UX

Predictable interactions, instant feedback, proper focus management, light and dark themes, reduced-motion and a11y from day one.

II.

Modularity

Every module is standalone — its own api.ts, its own tests, its own lazy popup. No cross-module imports, no shared state.

III.

Performance

Lazy tabs, hover-prefetching, nothing heavy on the popup-open path. The popup opens before you can blink.

First launch on macOS
The build isn't Apple-notarised yet (Apple Developer ID costs $99/yr; it's on the roadmap). On first open you'll see "Apple cannot verify…". Two ways through:
  1. Right-click Stash.app in /Applications → Open → confirm. macOS remembers it after that.
  2. Or one command in Terminal:
    xattr -d com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Stash.app
